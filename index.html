Privacy Policy & Architecture

At Skrit, privacy isn't an option—it is the default architecture. Unlike traditional platforms that monitor your online fingerprint, we do not track, log, or sell your search context history.

1. Local Device Storage Policies

Your data belongs to you. All user preference switches, interface memory states, and layout configurations are kept securely inside your individual computer or phone's web sandboxed browser memory (such as localStorage). This telemetry never leaves your system and cannot be read by external operators.

2. Zero Identity Mapping

We don't maintain user accounts or require sign-ins to look up information. Every query you execute passes dynamically through public index pipelines stripped completely of IP strings, physical geo-location data, cookie identifier packets, or custom user-agent trackers.

3. External Third-Party Media Requests

When utilizing embedded fallbacks to fetch direct real-time visual resources (such as Google Images/Videos or Wikipedia structures), connections are initiated direct-to-source on the client side. Your network queries are processed cleanly to keep you fully shielded from tracking arrays.
